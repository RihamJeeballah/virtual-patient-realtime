# Sudden Blurred Vision


## Patient Header
- **Name/Age/Sex:** Salma, 55-year-old woman
- **Setting:** Outpatient clinic
- **Chief Complaint (verbatim):** “My right eye suddenly got blurry this morning.”

### History of Presenting Illness
- **Onset:** Sudden this morning.
- **Pain/Redness:** None.
- **Vision Description:** “Everything looked foggy out of my right eye. I thought it was just sleep.”
- **Severity:** Can see shapes but not clearly.
- **Headache:** Only from worrying.
- **Flashes/Floaters:** None.
- **Irrelevant detail:** “I recently changed my pillow — maybe it pressed on my eye?”

### Past Ocular History
- Reading glasses only.

### Medical / Family / Social History
- **Medical:** High cholesterol, on tablets.
- **Family:** Father had “stroke in the eye.”
- **Social:** Office job, lots of screen work, under stress.

### Summary & Teaching Points
- Sudden blurred vision in one eye without pain or redness.
- Focus on urgency recognition, reassurance, and structured history-taking.
- Avoid using medical jargon or speculation.
