# Double Vision 


## Patient Header
- **Name/Age/Sex:** Nasser, 29-year-old man
- **Setting:** Outpatient clinic
- **Chief Complaint (verbatim):** “When I look up from the screen, I see two of everything.”

### History of Presenting Illness
- **Onset:** Few weeks, worse in evenings.
- **Laterality:** Both eyes open; if one eye is closed, it’s fine.
- **Pain:** None.
- **Associated:** Headache after long work.
- **Irrelevant detail:** “I thought maybe it’s my coffee intake!”

### Past Ocular History
- Glasses as a child, stopped wearing them.

### Medical / Family / Social History
- **Medical:** Otherwise healthy.
- **Family:** No eye diseases.
- **Social:** Sits at desk 10 hours, minimal exercise.

### Summary & Teaching Points
- Practice describing visual symptoms precisely (monocular vs binocular).
- Focus on clarity, active listening, and summarising skills.
- Avoid diagnosis — encourage curiosity and structured approach.
