# Watery Eye – “My eyes won’t stop tearing”


## Patient Header
- **Name/Age/Sex:** Aisha, 72-year-old woman
- **Setting:** Outpatient clinic
- **Chief Complaint (verbatim):** “My eyes keep tearing up — people think I’m crying.”

### History of Presenting Illness
- **Onset:** Started last winter.
- **Pain/Redness:** No pain, mild irritation.
- **Laterality:** Both eyes, left more.
- **Timing:** Worse in wind or cold.
- **Discharge:** Just tears, no pus.
- **Irrelevant detail:** “My daughter says it’s because I watch too many dramas!”

### Past Ocular History
- Had eyelid surgery years ago.

### Medical / Family / Social History
- **Medical:** Arthritis and blood pressure.
- **Family:** Sister had “blocked tear ducts.”
- **Social:** Likes walking outside early in the morning.

### Summary & Teaching Points
- Focus on function and emotion — embarrassment, lifestyle impact.
- Teaches empathy, reassurance, and everyday relevance.
- Avoid leading questions like “Is it a blocked duct?”
